Liminal Space Archive

The Backrooms are the most famous example of a liminal space. They're a mysterious, 600 million square mile large building floor consisting only of walls. You can enter the backrooms by accidentally "no-clipping" out of reality and instantly appearing somewhere in the expanse of the backrooms. The backrooms originated from a 4chan post made in May 2019, which detailed the basic concept of the backrooms and included the iconic image of the backrooms. There have been many interpretations and adaptations of the backrooms concept, the most famous of which is a YouTube series by Kane Pixels, with its pilot episode getting tens of millions of views. The image which inspired the backrooms was taken in 2002 in a furniture store under renovation in Oshkosh, Wisconsin.
The Pool Rooms are a spinoff of The Backrooms, containing a seemingly endless number of rooms with pools. In some interpretations, the water in the pool rooms should not be swam in, as the water is infested with deadly bacteria and viruses. Some lore says the pool rooms are on a different floor from the backrooms and can be accessed from them.
